# Doctrine: Bottleneck-First Analysis

**Always identify the physical or material bottleneck before recommending a proxy.**

## Why
In complex technological shifts (AI, CPO, ABF), the market often focuses on the "celebrity" stocks (e.g., NVIDIA) while ignoring the physical gatekeepers (e.g., Ajinomoto, Nittobo). Identifying the bottleneck allows for "Deep Dive" investing—finding monopolies with infinite pricing power that are hidden from the broader retail narrative.

## How to apply
1.  **Deconstruct the Hardware:** When a user asks about a new tech (e.g., "Rubin GPUs"), research the physical bill of materials.
2.  **Identify the Monopolies:** Search for "market share" + "material" (e.g., "T-glass market share"). Look for 80%+ concentration.
3.  **Validate the Squeeze:** Check utilization rates and price hike reports from these monopolies.
4.  **Recommend the "Tax Collector":** Prioritize the monopoly over the manufacturer if the manufacturer's Capex is too high.

## Counter-example
Recommending an AI server manufacturer (like Foxconn) without realizing they are currently waiting on a shortage of $50 substrate boards. You are betting on the "assembler" when the "board maker" owns the margin.
