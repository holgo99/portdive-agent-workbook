# Pattern: Triple-Lock Allocation

**Monopoly Material + Tech Leader + Proxy Torque.**

## Why
Direct investment in a bottleneck is often difficult due to liquidity or listing limitations (e.g., Unimicron not on TR). This pattern creates a "synthetic" exposure that covers all layers of the squeeze while maintaining liquidity on retail brokers.

## How to apply
When constructing an investment basket, use the **40/30/20/10** or **50/30/20** rule:
1.  **Monopoly Material (40-50%):** The "Tax Collector" who owns the IP/Material (e.g., Ajinomoto).
2.  **Tech Leader (30%):** The manufacturer with the highest yields and best customer list (e.g., Ibiden).
3.  **Hidden Component (20%):** The sub-bottleneck (e.g., Nittobo for T-glass).
4.  **Proxy Torque (10%):** A Western or secondary alternative with high sensitivity to the catalyst (e.g., AT&S).

## Example
**ABF Thesis Allocation:**
*   40% Ajinomoto (Film Monopoly)
*   30% Ibiden (Substrate Leader)
*   20% Nittobo (T-Glass Monopoly)
*   10% AT&S (Western Capacity)
